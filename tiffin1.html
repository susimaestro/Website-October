Tiffin Café -
E-commerce responsive webpage

Tiffin is a family run café in the center of Lisbon serving plant based food made with love. They wanted to increase their online visibility with an E-commerce site, but on their own terms making sure the style felt like them.

CASE SUMMARY

Problem

How can we help them increase their online precense in a way that they feel correctly represented?

Goal

The goal was to design a web page that had their core values of honesty, authenticity and community at heart.

Solution

The solution was a simple and straightforward web page reflecting all the values that were important to the café

Process highlights

My role

We were three UX designer doing the user research, then my role was to take care of the research, ideating, branding, design, prototypes and preparing assets for the developer ..

Timeline

April- May 2020 using the Agile method and delivering protoypes for mobile, tablet and desktop

Tools

Google forms, Adobe XD, Figma, Photoshop & Illustrator

Defining & Ideation

Now I had to examine the information gathered in the user research period and organize everything to get an overview. In order to empathize with the users to the maximum I used various tools like affinity map, user journeys, user personas and user stories to put myself in their shoes.

By looking at the users pain points, what the competitors were doing, the brainstorming phase was kicked off with the leading question 'How might we create an E-commerce website for Tiffin that reflects their authentic and honest values?'

Prototyping & Iteration

Now for the tricky part - Validating the design solutions with the users through prototypes! To get into it quickly and to save myself from potentially wasting time designing I started with a Low-fi prototype to see if the framework, my intention and navigation made sense and was intuitive. The users gave some feedback which was implemented for the Mid-fi prototype which was tested on Useberry, an amazing platform where you can upload your prototype with a task for the users to complete. It records all their actions so you can see where they got confused. Pretty handy! From there follows more iterations and improvements untill we move on to the Hi-fidelity prototype

VISUALS

Establishing a brand identity for Tiffin was next on the list. I took into account all the keywords that kept appearing when interviewing both the owners and their customer; Authentic, healthy, warm, natural, human, a home away from home.. I took a great deal of inspiration from the look of the real Tiffin Café, a place with a of wooden textures, plants, family board games and pictures of Tiffin's story and the community up on the wall. In the beginning we started with this beige pink version below but ended up with the green as it represented their vegeterian menu well.

Learnings

In this project I learned the value of effecient teamwork. In the beginning phase we worked so well togheter that we did a ton of research in only a couple of days. The team spirit really kept us motivated and I really missed that aspect once I was working alone.
