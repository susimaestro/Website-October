NOS Play -
On-boarding &
Redesign

NOS is the leading entertainment group in Portugal and NOS Play is one of their streaming services. The client wanted to create an onboarding experience for their mobile and web app in hopes to engage their customers to use the service more. After the user research it was understood a redesign was also in order to suit their needs

CASE SUMMARY

Problem

The problem was that NOS customers were not really using the app because they found the app tedious, confusing and difficult to navigate through.

Goal

The goal was to make the service more engaging, intuitive and improve menu navigation

Solution

Solutions included static menus with simplified options and layout + engaging features like customizable lists and the option to connect to social media

Process highlights

My role

UX/UI designer responsible for conducting user research, development, creating the onboarding experience and the redesign.

Timeline

May- June 2020 using the Agile method and delivering prototypes

Tools

Google forms, Adobe XD, Photoshop & Illustrator

Research

The research can be divided into four categories; Desk, User, Market and Competitive research. My focus was to get to know NOS and their users to huss out pain points, frustrations and room for improvement. Through the user and competitive research I could get a better idea of the users expectation of a streaming service, and also be able to see where the gaps where to brainstorm design opportunities!

Defining & Ideation

It was time to inspect all the information gathered in the user research period and organize and classify everything to get an overview. In order to empathize with the users to the maxinum I used various tools like affinity map, user journeys, user personas and user stories to put myself in their shoes.

By looking at the users pain points, what the competitors were doing and what was missing on the market, a How might we..? question was formed and lead the way during the brainstorming phase

Prototyping & Iteration

Now for the tricky part - Validating the design solutions with the users through prototypes! To get into it quickly and to save myself from potentially wasting time designing I started with a Low-fi prototype to see if the framework, my intention and navigation made sense and was intuitive. The users gave some feedback which was implemented for the Mid-fi prototype which was tested on Useberry, an amazing platform where you can upload your prototype with a task for the users to complete. It records all their actions so you can see where they got confused. Pretty handy! From there follows more iterations and improvements untill we move on to the Hi-fidelity prototype

Learnings

When I started with this project I was very unsure on how to tackle it, which in the beginning left me a bit flustered as usually I have some idea of what the end results will be. I expected to design an onboarding experience, but ended up redesigning the platform as well because of what the users told me in the research phase. I let the research led the way and decide which path to take and learnt to trust the process!
